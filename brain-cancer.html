Comprehensive care for lung cancer

We manage primary lung cancers and metastatic disease involving the lungs. Each case is reviewed in a multidisciplinary tumor board to guide staging, molecular testing and individualized treatment planning.

  • Advanced CT and PET-CT imaging, bronchoscopy and guided biopsy for accurate staging.
  • Molecular profiling to identify actionable biomarkers for targeted and immunotherapy.
  • Close coordination between thoracic surgery, medical and radiation oncology for multimodality care.

Surgery & local therapies

Our thoracic surgeons perform lung-sparing and oncologic resections with minimally invasive approaches when appropriate.

  • Lobectomy, segmentectomy and sleeve resections via VATS or robotic approaches.
  • Complex chest wall and mediastinal tumor resections when indicated.
  • Stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) as a non-surgical curative option for selected early-stage patients.

Systemic therapy & supportive care

Medical oncology and pulmonary teams support patients through systemic treatment and recovery.

  • Chemotherapy, targeted therapies and immunotherapy tailored to tumor biology.
  • Symptom management including pulmonary care and oxygen support when needed.
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation, smoking cessation, and psychosocial support.

Long-term follow-up

We provide structured follow-up and monitoring with regular imaging.

  • Scheduled MRI scans and neurological assessments.
  • Management of late effects such as fatigue or cognitive changes.
  • Return-to-work, driving and lifestyle guidance.

When to seek urgent evaluation

Please seek immediate medical attention if you notice:

  • New or worsening severe headaches, especially with nausea or vomiting.
  • New seizures or convulsions.
  • Weakness, numbness or difficulty speaking.
  • Sudden changes in vision, balance or personality.
