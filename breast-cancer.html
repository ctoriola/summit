How we care for patients with breast cancer

Every patient’s breast cancer is unique. We tailor investigations and treatment plans based on tumor biology, stage, overall health and personal preferences, following international guidelines and evidence-based protocols.

  • Dedicated breast clinics for rapid evaluation of new breast symptoms.
  • Advanced imaging including digital mammography, ultrasound and MRI.
  • Image-guided biopsy and pathology review with receptor and genomic testing.

Surgery & local control

Our surgical oncologists offer the full spectrum of breast surgeries.

  • Breast-conserving surgery (lumpectomy) when appropriate.
  • Mastectomy with or without immediate reconstruction.
  • Sentinel lymph node biopsy and axillary surgery.

Systemic therapy & radiation

Medical and radiation oncologists collaborate to plan the safest, most effective treatments.

  • Neoadjuvant and adjuvant chemotherapy.
  • Hormonal therapy for hormone-receptor-positive disease.
  • HER2-targeted and other novel agents when indicated.
  • Modern radiation techniques to protect heart and lungs.

Survivorship and long-term follow up

Care continues even after active treatment is complete.

  • Personalized follow-up schedule and imaging plan.
  • Lymphedema prevention and management.
  • Rehabilitation, nutrition and psychosocial support.

When to see a breast specialist

Please seek evaluation if you notice any of the following:

  • Lump or thickening in the breast or underarm.
  • Change in breast shape, size or skin (dimpling, redness, scaling).
  • Nipple discharge, inversion or new pain that persists.
  • Abnormal findings on a screening mammogram.
