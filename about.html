Our Mission

Here’s what drives us every single day

Every Nigerian dealing with cancer deserves world-class care. Right here at home. Not in India. Not in South Africa. Here. We combine the treatment standards you’d find at top international cancer centers with doctors and navigators who understand your culture and a team that treats you like family.

What we fix

No more draining savings to travel abroad. No more abandoning treatment halfway because the system makes it too hard. No more navigating a fragmented maze alone. At Summit, you get evidence-based treatment delivered with genuine compassion, a patient navigator who checks in, and doctors who explain things in plain language.

Core Values

  • We Don’t Cut Corners

    Excellence is the baseline: cleanroom pharmacy standards, evidence-based protocols, and relentless attention to safety.

  • Compassion Isn’t a Buzzword Here

    We treat people as people—receptionists learn names, nurses listen, and our financial counselor fights for coverage.

  • You’re a Person, Not a Case Number

    Decisions start with what’s best for YOU—your family, your work, your life—not convenience or protocol alone.

  • We Respect Your Culture

    We honor religious observances, family decision-making styles, and safe traditional practices when appropriate.

  • We Tell You Straight

    Clear pricing, honest prognosis conversations, and plain-language explanations are how we earn your trust.

  • We Stay Sharp

    Our team attends international conferences and maintains active memberships so your care benefits from the latest evidence.

Our Story

How Summit Started

Summit Medical Services began when three doctors stopped asking "why" and started building a solution that kept patients in Nigeria, cared for by teams who understand them.

Dr. Shagaya Uchechukwu remembers a woman diagnosed with Stage II breast cancer—completely treatable—who abandoned treatment because travel costs and access made it impossible to continue. Dr. Nwachukwu Emmanuella and Prof. Chiedozi saw the same stories repeatedly: patients who should have survived, lost to a fragmented system.

In 2025, they stopped complaining and started building. Summit opened in Mambila Barracks, Asokoro—close to central Abuja and easy to reach. They built a cleanroom pharmacy, comfortable infusion chairs, and hired nurses who listen. Most importantly, they built a team that refuses to let patients disappear: navigators who send reminders, financial counselors who help secure coverage, and doctors who explain treatment plans until patients understand.

“For the first time since my diagnosis, I don’t feel alone.” — a recent patient

That’s what Summit is. You’re not alone anymore.
